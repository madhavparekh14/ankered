System Overview

Three layers.
One decision.

Ankered fuses orientation tracking and load-bearing data to make collapse-prevention decisions faster than the human nervous system can react.

01

Orientation Sensing

A 3-axis magnetometer mounted on the shin continuously measures lower limb orientation, sampling at high frequency to build a precise angular velocity profile of knee motion throughout each step cycle.

Magnetometer · IMU
02

Ground Contact Detection

Pressure sensors embedded in the shoe sole determine exactly when and where the foot is bearing weight — providing the crucial context that distinguishes a dangerous buckling event from an intentional controlled movement.

Plantar Pressure Array
03

Reactive Deployment

When the system detects a rapid transition from near extension into uncontrolled flexion — within a 0° to 60° range across a 50 to 150 ms window while under load — deployable stabilizers lock within the brace to arrest the collapse and maintain structural integrity.

Deployable Stabilizers

Collapse Prevention

Sub-reflex Response Time

Human voluntary motor response averages 150–250ms. Ankered's mechanical deployment is engineered to complete within the 50–150ms detection window — acting before conscious intervention is possible.

Dual-Modal Confirmation

Angular velocity alone is insufficient. Ankered cross-references orientation data with real-time plantar pressure to confirm active weight-bearing — eliminating false positives during seated or passive leg movement.

Passive Safety Architecture

The system's default state is unlocked and unintrusive, allowing full range of motion during normal gait. Stabilizers only engage when the specific biomechanical signature of uncontrolled collapse is detected.

Continuous Orientation Mapping

The magnetometer builds a continuous model of limb position throughout the gait cycle, enabling the system to distinguish between similar angular changes that are normal (controlled stepping) versus dangerous (buckling).

Applications

Who Ankered
protects.

Knee instability affects millions across clinical, athletic, and occupational contexts. Ankered's platform adapts to each environment.

01 / CLINICAL

Post-Surgical Rehabilitation

ACL reconstruction, total knee replacement, and meniscal repair patients face elevated collapse risk during early mobilization. Ankered provides an active safety layer that supports recovery without restricting therapeutic movement.

Rehab · Orthopaedics
02 / NEUROLOGICAL

Neuromuscular Conditions

Patients with MS, stroke-related hemiplegia, Parkinson's disease, and peripheral neuropathy experience unpredictable episodes of knee buckling. Ankered operates as a constant mechanical safety net — providing confidence and fall prevention in daily life.

MS · Stroke · Parkinson's
03 / ATHLETIC

Return-to-Sport Confidence

Athletes returning from knee injury face psychological barriers alongside physical ones. Ankered's real-time protection enables athletes to push through rehabilitation thresholds knowing a mechanical safeguard is active during every training session.
